Most widely used today. The signal is transmitted from the camera to the DVR using the TCP/IP protocol over the Ethernet network.

An IP video surveillance system consists of:

  • IP Video camera.
  • Network Video Recorder (NVR).
  • Network switch.
  • Auxiliary equipment.

The signal is digitized on the video camera , which converts the signal for transmission over an Ethernet network.

Network Video Recorder or NVR ) are designed to work in IP video surveillance systems. Unlike conventional DVRs, NVRs receive compressed video data over an Ethernet network. A feature of NVR is that they can fully work only with a limited list of IP video camera models that have been integrated into the DVR software. At the same time, uniform standards for video surveillance systems developed by the ONVIF forum are widespread.

ONVIF – Open Network Video Interface Forum — an international industry organization that develops standardized protocols for the interaction of various equipment and software that make up security systems. ONVIF developers have chosen the most ready-made technologies and adapted them for IP video surveillance. In particular, the ONVIF specifications are built on modern web services described by the WSDL language, RTP/RTSP, SOAP (XML) protocols, H.264, MPEG-4, MJPEG video compression standards.

ONVIF's primary video transmission is RTSP protocol. The Real Time Streaming Protocol (RTSP), developed by the IETF in 1998 and described in RFC 2326, is an application protocol for use in multimedia systems that allows a client to remotely control the stream of data from a server by providing the ability to execute commands, such as "Start", "Stop", as well as time access to files located on the server.

The request is sent to the server as text in format: "absolute_address method content protocol_version". Along with the request, additional service fields can be transferred (on new lines of the request).

Query example:

"PLAY rtsp: //server/path/test.mpg RTSP/1.0"

Some NVR models Also have built-in network switch for direct connection of PoE-enabled cameras.

Power over Ethernet (PoE) — a technology that allows the transfer of electrical energy, along with data, to a remote device of a tasty treat, via a standard twisted-pair cable in an Ethernet network. PoE is described by IEEE 802.3af-2003 and IEEE 802.3at-2009. 

Based on IEEE 802.3af , provides up to 400 mA DC at 48 V (36 to 57V) nominal voltage through two pairs of conductors in a four-pair cable to deliver a maximum power of 15.4 W

Standard IEEE 802.3at- 2009, also known as PoE+ or PoE plus, delivers up to 25.5W of power. However, some manufacturers announced the release of devices that consume power in all pairs and thus receive power up to & nbsp; 60 W.

Benefits of IP CCTV systems:

  • Unlimited maximum resolution of video cameras.
  • Architecture flexibility and scalability.
  • The ability to connect remote cameras via the Internet.
  • Implementation of video analytics functions on video cameras.

Disadvantages of IP CCTV systems:

  • High load on the local network, which may require the creation of a separate video surveillance network.
  • High volume of video surveillance archive.
  • Proprietary formats of data transfer protocols from video cameras.
  • The equipment is expensive.

 

A number of requirements apply to IP video surveillance systems TNLA, according to which certain conditions are imposed when designing and installing video surveillance systems at public safety facilities and crowded places.
