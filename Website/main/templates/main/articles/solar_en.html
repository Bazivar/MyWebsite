For 12V batteries, the required Ah capacity is 124 Ah.

The inverter requires an input voltage of at least 48V to operate, i.e. four batteries connected in series are required. The capacity of each battery must be at least 31 Ah. The closest standard value for 12V batteries will be 40Ah. We use DT 1240 batteries from Delta.

The solar module requires both consumer power and battery charging at the same time. Powering the consumer will require 100W of output power, while charging the batteries will also require additional solar cell power. In the case of using a DT 1240 battery, the optimal charging current per battery will be 4A (10% of the ampere-hour capacity), which, at a voltage of 48V, will require 192 W of charge power. In this case, the battery will be charged for at least 10 hours, which fits into the average daylight hours.

Thus, the required minimum output power of solar modules should be at least 292W.

To operate the system at 48V low voltage, at least two 24V solar panels connected in series are required.

Taking into account the average daily solar flux of 118.75 W/m2, as well as the average solar module efficiency of about 17%, we get that to ensure output power of at least 292 W, you will need a solar module with an area S of at least:

 

S = 292 / 118.75 0.17 = 14.46 m2

 

That is, when using two solar modules with a voltage of 24V each, the area of solar module should be about 7.3 m2

Due to the fact that a solar module of such area is not produced, it is required to configure two assemblies of parallel connected solar modules. The assemblies in this case will have an output voltage of 24V each and you will need to connect the assemblies in series.
